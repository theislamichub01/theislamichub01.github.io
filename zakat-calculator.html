What is Zakat?

Zakat is one of the five pillars of Islam. It is an obligatory charity that Muslims must pay annually on their wealth and assets that have been in their possession for a full lunar year.

The standard Zakat rate is 2.5% of your total qualifying wealth.

Who must pay Zakat?

  • Adult Muslims who are sane
  • Free (not slaves)
  • In possession of the minimum amount of wealth (Nisab) for one lunar year

Nisab: The minimum amount of wealth required before Zakat is due. It is equivalent to 85 grams of gold or 595 grams of silver.

Important Notes

This calculator provides an estimate. For precise calculations and specific situations, please consult with a qualified Islamic scholar or your local mosque.

Assets NOT subject to Zakat:

  • Your primary residence
  • Personal car(s) for transportation
  • Household furniture and appliances
  • Personal clothing and jewelry worn regularly
  • Tools and equipment needed for your profession

When to pay Zakat: Zakat should be paid as soon as it becomes due. You can calculate your Zakat date based on when you first became eligible or use a fixed date like Ramadan each year.
